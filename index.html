Formula & Explanation

Formula for R20

R20 = RT / [1 + α(T - 20)]

R20: Resistance at 20°C

RT: Measured resistance at temperature T

α: Temperature coefficient of resistance

T: Temperature in °C at measurement

Material Coefficients (α)

EC (ECAL) α = 0.00403
Alloy (AAAC) α = 0.0036
Al59 (ALS9) α = 0.0038

How It Works

Electrical resistance changes with temperature. This calculator normalizes resistance measurements to the standard reference temperature of 20°C using the linear temperature coefficient of resistance (α).

Select your material, input the measured resistance and temperature, and the calculator will provide the normalized resistance value at 20°C.
